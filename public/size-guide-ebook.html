If you have ever ordered your "usual" size and couldn't even button it up, it is not your fault. The fashion industry's math is completely broken.

Clothing sizes are mathematically scaled up from one single sample size. Every brand defines sizes differently. A size Medium at Zara has a completely different measurement than a Medium at COS.

Your size did not change. The brand changed the definition.

To stop wasting money on return shipping, you have to stop trusting the letter on the tag and start using your actual data.

Your True Fit Identity

To map your body to any brand's size chart, you only need to know these 6 exact measurements:

  • Shoulder — The width from the edge of one shoulder bone to the other. The one measurement that makes or breaks every jacket.
  • Chest / Bust — The circumference around the fullest part.
  • Waist — The narrowest part of your torso, usually just above the belly button.
  • Hips — The fullest part of your lower body.
  • Inseam — The distance from the top of your inner thigh down to your ankle.
  • Sleeve — From the top of your shoulder down to your wrist bone.

Pro-Tip: Keep these numbers written in your phone's Notes app and check them against a retailer's size chart before every single purchase.

The 3-Question
Checkout Checklist

Before you hit "Complete Purchase," run through this list to guarantee a zero-return order:

✔

Did I check my exact measurements against the brand's specific size chart?

Never assume you are a Medium just because you are a Medium somewhere else. Cross-reference your 6 numbers with their specific chart.

✔

What is the fabric composition?

Look at the materials. Does it have elastane/spandex (meaning it will stretch and forgive), or is it 100% rigid cotton (meaning you might need to size up for comfort)?

✔

Have I seen this on my exact body type?

Buying clothes based on a 6'2" fashion model is a data illusion. Seek out reviews or photos of the garment on people who share your height and proportions.
